On creating a campaign

Introduction

This is a short essay on what's expected within a RTS-game's campaign. This is not professional thing - just some thought of mine. But I think they should be considered when planning a good campaign.

Consider this as some ideas and if you have any ideas how this document can be improved. Write it down and send it to me. I will include it.

Guidelines

Here are some ideas about creating good campaigns for a RTS-game in general.

  • Geography

    Before starting a campaign the country-side, some cities or villages should be thougt of and named.

  • People

    Important people that will have some appearance within a campaign should be introduced. What's really important here is that the player has to care for this characters. So these shouldn't be introduced at a too late stage of the game. Otherwise the player asks himself - who's this guy and why the xxxx shall I deal with this?

  • Story

    The story must be reasonable - there's nothing more annoying than inconsistent storylines.

  • Varying gameplay

    The actual gameplay must vary a little - otherwise it'll get boring even if there's an interesting story. Within standard RTS-games this may be a) build base, b) attack enemy with full force, c) spy & conquer. As you can see this is not very varying. This is why BoA will try to include many different faces of the strategy genre. More on this can be found in the gameplay section.

  • Varying difficulty

    The difficulty level must increase within a campaign. NO matter how difficult or easy it should be on a whole. You may include some easier parts as present for a fulfilled hard job. But on the whole the difficulty must increase.

Because BoA is no typical RTS-game, we have to deal with the specialties. If you're not yet familiar with BoA's gameplay or its specialties continue within the game-description.
